How to Fill the Assessment Form

📋 General Guidelines

Be Honest and Accurate

The AI model's accuracy depends on honest responses. Don't try to "game" the system — provide truthful information about your health status.

Recent Information

Use your most recent health information. If you've had recent tests or doctor visits, base your answers on those results.

When in Doubt

If you're unsure about a medical condition, choose "Yes" if you've ever been diagnosed with it, even if it's currently controlled.

🩺 Medical History Tips

High Blood Pressure

Select "Yes" if you've been diagnosed with hypertension or take blood pressure medication, even if it's currently controlled.

High Cholesterol

Choose "Yes" if your total cholesterol is above 240 mg/dL or if you take cholesterol medication.

BMI Calculation

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². Use the built-in "Calculate BMI" button in the form for easy calculation.

Diabetes Status

Choose "Pre-diabetes" if your A1C is 5.7–6.4%, "Yes" if A1C ≥ 6.5% or you take diabetes medication.

💪 Lifestyle Factors

Physical Activity

Select "Yes" if you did ANY physical activity in the past 30 days — walking, sports, gym, household work, etc.

Fruit & Vegetable Consumption

Count ALL forms — fresh, frozen, canned, dried, and 100% juice. One serving = 1 medium fruit or ½ cup vegetables.

Heavy Alcohol Consumption

For men: >14 drinks/week or >4 drinks/day. For women: >7 drinks/week or >3 drinks/day.

Smoking Status

Select "Smoker" if you currently smoke or have smoked within the past year, regardless of frequency.

🏥 Health Status Guidelines

General Health Rating

Consider your overall health compared to others your age. Think about energy levels, chronic conditions, and daily functioning.

Mental Health Days

Count days with stress, depression, anxiety, or emotional problems that affected your daily activities. Enter 0–30.

Physical Health Days

Count days with physical illness, injury, or health problems that limited your activities. Enter 0–30.

Difficulty Walking

Consider if you have serious difficulty walking or climbing stairs due to health problems, not just being out of shape.

🚨 Medical Disclaimer

This assessment tool is designed for educational and screening purposes only. It should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for proper medical evaluation and care.

Parameter Information & Guidelines

High Blood Pressure

Indicates if you have been diagnosed with high blood pressure (hypertension). This is a major risk factor for heart disease.

High Cholesterol

Shows if you have high cholesterol levels. High cholesterol can lead to plaque buildup in arteries, increasing heart disease risk.

Cholesterol Check

Whether you've had your cholesterol checked in the past 5 years. Regular monitoring is important for heart health.

Body Mass Index (BMI)

A measure of body fat based on height and weight. Normal BMI: 18.5–24.9, Overweight: 25–29.9, Obese: 30+.

Smoking Status

Current or former smoking habits. Smoking significantly increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Previous Stroke

History of stroke indicates higher risk for future cardiovascular events including heart attacks.

Diabetes Status

Diabetes significantly increases heart disease risk. Pre-diabetes is also a risk factor that should be monitored.

Physical Activity

Regular physical activity in the past 30 days. Exercise is protective against heart disease.

Fruit Consumption

Daily fruit intake (1+ servings per day). Fruits provide antioxidants and nutrients that support heart health.

Vegetable Consumption

Daily vegetable intake (1+ servings per day). Vegetables are rich in heart-healthy nutrients and fiber.

Heavy Alcohol Consumption

Excessive alcohol intake can increase blood pressure and contribute to heart disease risk.

Healthcare Coverage

Access to healthcare services affects early detection and management of heart disease risk factors.

Healthcare Cost Barriers

Financial barriers to healthcare can prevent proper monitoring and treatment of heart disease risk factors.

General Health

Overall self-reported health status from excellent to poor. General health perception correlates with actual health outcomes.

Mental Health Days

Number of days in the past 30 days when mental health was not good. Mental health affects overall cardiovascular health.

Physical Health Days

Number of days in the past 30 days when physical health was not good. This reflects overall health status.

Difficulty Walking

Serious difficulty walking or climbing stairs. This can indicate mobility issues related to cardiovascular health.

Gender

Biological sex affects heart disease risk patterns. Men generally have higher risk at younger ages.

Age

Age is a significant risk factor for heart disease. Risk increases with advancing age for both men and women.
